Step-by-Step Breakdown

1. Minimum Vertical Jump Required to Dunk

Formula:
Hoop Height = Standing Reach + Palm Size

This calculation tells you how much vertical jump is needed to dunk. It compares your standing reach with the basketball rim height. A useful feature for those searching "how much vert to dunk" or "vertical needed to dunk calculator".

2. Total Dunk Reach

Formula:
Standing Reach + Vertical Jump

This helps calculate your total jump height — i.e., how high you can reach when you jump. Perfect for anyone looking for a "dunk height calculator" or "dunking calculator".

3. Jumping Energy Calculation

Formula:
Energy = Mass × Gravity × Height

Using classical physics, we determine how much muscle energy is required to perform the dunk. Ideal for athletes and trainers analyzing power output.

4. Hang Time Estimation

Hang time is the duration you remain in the air after takeoff. This hang time vertical jump calculator gives you an estimate based on your jump height, showcasing real-time air-time.

5. Takeoff Velocity

This shows how fast you leave the ground when starting the jump. It's a great way to understand your explosiveness and track athletic performance.

How Much Vertical Do You Need to Dunk at 6 Feet Tall?

If you're 6 feet (183 cm) tall, you typically need a vertical jump of around 28 to 34 inches to dunk a basketball.

Here's Why:

A standard basketball rim is 10 feet (120 inches) high.

The average standing reach for a 6-foot person is about 7 feet 9 inches (93 inches).

To dunk, your hand needs to go about 6 inches above the rim (for control and clearance).

That means you must reach at least 10 feet 6 inches, which requires jumping about 30 to 34 inches vertically.
